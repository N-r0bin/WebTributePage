Malcom X

American Muslim minister and human rights activist who was a prominent figure during the civil rights movement.

Time line of Malcom X's life:

  • 1925 - Malcom Little is born in Omaha, NE

  • 1929 - The family's Lansing home is burned.

  • 1931 - Malcom's father found dead on the town's trolley tracks.

  • 1946 - Malcom is sentenced to 8-10 years for armed robbery and serves 6 years at Charlestown, MA State Prison.

  • 1948-49 - While in prision converts to islam.

  • 1953 - Changes name from Malcolm Little to Malcolm X and becomes Assistant Minister of Nation of Islam’s Detroit Temple.

  • 1954 - Promoted to Minister of Nation of Islam’s New York Temple.

  • 1958 - Marries Sister Betty X in Lansing, Michigan.

  • 1959 - Travels to Middle East and Africa.

  • 1963 - Nation of Islam orders Malcolm X to be silent, allegedly because of remarks concerning President Kennedy’s assassination

  • 1964 March - Malcolm X leaves the Nation of Islam and starts his new organization, Muslim Mosque, Inc.

  • 1964 April - Travels to Middle East and Africa.

  • 1964 May - Starts the Organization of Afro-American Unity (OAAU), a secular political group.

  • Feburary 14, 1965 - Malcolm X’s home is firebombed.

  • Feburary 21, 1965 - Malcolm X is assassinated as he begins speaking at the Audubon Ballroom, New York.
